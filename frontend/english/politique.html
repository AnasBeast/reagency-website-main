WHAT IS A COOKIE?

In short, a cookie is a small text file that is placed on your computer or mobile device by your Internet browser. They are used by many websites and normally do not identify you personally. The cookie allows the website to remember your actions or preferences over time. Most browsers support cookies, but you can modify your browser to refuse them and you can
delete them at any time.

WHY DO WE USE COOKIES?

Cookies allow us to improve the content, functionality, speed and usability of the website. Their tracking ability also helps us understand your preferences, so you don't need to provide the same information every visit or multiple times during a visit.

HOW WE USE COOKIES?

This site uses different types of cookies: Technical cookies. They are essential for the proper functioning of the site. Functional cookies. They are
necessary to provide the services requested by users on the site. Analytical cookies. They allow us to see how visitors use the website and to record the content they view that is of interest to them. This helps us improve the service we provide by ensuring that our
users find what they are looking for.
Social cookies. They allow us to provide functionalities relating to social networks (Facebook, Google and Twitter). Affiliate cookies. They allow us to track visits to our affiliate websites. Advertising cookies. They collect information about your preferences and choices on the site. They target advertising networks, which use them to show you personalized advertisements on other websites.

HOW TO CONTROL COOKIES?

You can control and/or delete cookies at any time. For more details, see www.aboutcookies.org. Cookie preferences must be set for each browser (Internet Explorer, Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox or Safari). You usually have several ways to manage cookies, such as enabling or completely disabling cookies, deleting certain cookies that are already stored in
e browser, blocking certain websites from storing cookies on your computer or blocking third-party cookies (cookies stored on your computer by another website). If you do, some parts of the website may not work.
